BRIYANI

Biryani (/bɜːrˈjɑːni/) is a mixed rice dish most popular in South Asia and was thought to have originated from ancient Iran.[1] It is made with rice, some type of meat (chicken, beef, goat, lamb, prawn, or fish) and spices. To cater to vegetarians, in some cases, it is prepared by substituting vegetables for the meat.[2] Sometimes eggs and/or potatoes are added.[3]

Biryani, a beloved Indian rice dish, has a fascinating history. It is believed to have originated in Iran and then traveled to the Indian subcontinent during the Mughal era. Over time, it adapted and evolved, becoming a culinary masterpiece. The irresistible flavors and aromatic spices made it a favorite among food enthusiasts.

Biryani is one of the most popular dishes in South Asia and among the South Asian diaspora, although the dish is often associated with the region’s Muslim population in particular.[4] Similar dishes are also prepared in other parts of the world such as in Iraq, Myanmar, Thailand, and Malaysia.[5] Biryani is the single most-ordered dish on Indian online food ordering and delivery services, and has been labelled as the most popular dish overall in India.[4][6]
